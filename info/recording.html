Here's a quick rundown on the various recording methods we used in our groundbreaking album!!!

studios

We used 4 tape recorders in various songs. The first one, used in the majority of songs was an Amstrad cassette recorder, (called "MF"!!) with the erase head turned permantently off!! The second was a mono Binatone portable tape recorder!!! (Called "Monotone"!!) The third was a most sophisticated Sony tape recorder, (Called "M8"!!!) where we could turn the erase head on and off!!! And the fourth and final one was a really swish Teac model (called "DJ"!!!) where we could mix in audio input and microphones in realtime!!!

So there are 5 types of recording setup used here:

setup 1 "MF" used with two microphones (used for most of the 1986 recordings)
setup 2 "Monotone" using one mic, then fiddled about with on "M8". (Some of the 1986 stuff)
setup 3 "MF" used with a pair of old headphones used as stereo mics!! (used for most of the 1987 recordings)
setup 4 Stuff recorded on "M8" (Some 1987 stuff)
setup 5 Stuff recorded on "DJ" (Some 1987 stuff)

instruments

casio vl-tone a fabby-dabby electronic instrument which look and acts like a calculator!! only it sounds better!!!
yamaha vss100 a marvel of modern electronics! it's an organ! it has loads of really cool synth sounds! and it has a secret weapon- an inbuilt sampler!
tin whistles no hey nonny-nonny stuff played with this tho!!!!
guitar it may be only acoustic and 6 string (it used to be 12 string, but it was cut back by the Tories!!!!!) but it doesn't sound like any guitar you've ever heard!!!
drums the cheapest drums ever to make such a big drum sound!! bet Phil Collins couldn't make such a great sound with cardboard boxes for drums and biros for drumsticks!!!!
"grunge" vocoder for the song "cat", PritStik invented a completely new instrument!!!! this special "grunge" vocoder was made from one of those mini-headphones you used to get on personal hifi's before the electrix ear-plugs came into vogue!! We used the left headphone as a vocoder "transducer", and it gave that special "grungy" vocoder effect you get in old Kraftwerk records!!!!

production style

we really liked a lot of those old 60s records where loads of the stuff was in the left channel and all the other stuff was in the right hand channel!!! So most of our recordings are produced in that very style!!!!

We also recorded nearly everything using microphones, even the electronic instruments, so we could get that authentic "live" sound! And we used different types of mics to get different sounds!!!! We even used old audio headphones and personal stereo headphones as mics!!!!

Even though we used "old-style" recording techniques and instruments, we still used up-to-date equipment like the VL-Tone and samplers!!! And we also pushed our analogue tape recorders to their limits in re-inventing the recording process as we went along!!!
